The Poet's Manuscript

“The Birdmer of the Dominion” From sunlit spires of Summerset gold, When banners sleep and war drums slow, A shadow lifts on silent wing, Where moonflowers and war horns grow. They call her Birdmer, feather-bold, Eyes like dawn on Elven steel, She dives through fire, through clash and cry, For the Pact to break, for the Dominion to heal. Clad in yellow, sworn to light, She laughs at arrows, spits at fear, Where lines collapse and hope runs thin, You hear the rush of wings draw near. No throne she seeks, no crown she claims, Just sky and song and victory won, So raise your glass when the night takes flight, To the Birdmer of the Dominion.

The Tavern Ballad
