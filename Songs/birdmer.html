The Poet's Manuscript


“The Birdmer of the Dominion” // unkown poet

From sunlit spires of Summerset gold, When banners sleep and war drums slow, A shadow lifts on silent wing, Where moonflowers and war horns grow.

They call her Birdmer, feather-bold, Eyes like dawn on Elven steel, She dives through fire, through clash and cry, For the Pact to break, for the Dominion to heal.

Clad in yellow, sworn to light, She laughs at arrows, spits at fear, Where lines collapse and hope runs thin, You hear the rush of wings draw near.

No throne she seeks, no crown she claims, Just sky and song and victory won, So raise your glass when the night takes flight, To the Birdmer of the Dominion.

The Tavern Ballad


“The Birdmer of the Dominion” // sang in taverns by bards

In a tavern south of Vulkhel Guard, Where the floor’s all sticky, the jokes are hard, There’s a song we sing when the wine runs low, ‘Bout a lunatic lass with wings to go.

She’s the Birdmer, squawk and shout, Trips on stools but still jumps out, Swears she meant to dive that way, Straight through a door and into a fray.

Feathers singed and boots on fire, Missed her target, hit the spire, “TACTICAL!” she proudly cries, As she faceplants from the skies.

So drink it down, don’t ask her why, She owes the bar, she’ll never fly… But when the brawl spills into the street, The Birdmer’s wings still find their beat.
